Introduction

The concept of Internet service providers providing all content and applications regardless of the source, and without any kind of discrimination to any particular product or website is called net neutrality. This is a complex issue and is related with social, political ethical and historical contexts. In this article, I am going to describe some concepts related to net neutrality, some of the common ways of hindering net neutrality , discuss some political positions regarding net neutrality, give an overview of some important figures and cases related to net neutrality and lastly discuss the ethical implications of this concept.

Common Carrier

A common carrier, in telecommunications, is an entity that provides wired and wireless communication services to the general public for a fee. A common carrier can be contrasted with a contract carrier, also called a private carrier, which provides services to a limited number of customers.
Because common carriers serve the general public, they are licensed by a regulatory body. In the United States, the common carrier designation made by the Federal Communications Commission (FCC), under authorization of the Telecommunications Act of 1934. One reason common carriers are licensed is to ensure they provide indiscriminate public access and protect the privacy of the general public. Whether or not the FCC has the legal right to declare that internet service providers (ISPs) are common carriers is a major consideration in the net neutrality debate. [1]

Communication Service vs. Utility

So to ensure net neutrality by law, it boils down to whether internet is defined as a common carrier or not. In 2002, the Federal communications Commission classified broadband internet providers as Information Services (Title I) which allowed relaxed regulations of the internet. But in 2015, Tom Wheeler, chairman of FCC reclassified broadband providers as a public utility under Title II to enforce open internet order and net neutrality. So the debate becomes whether broadband should be considered as a communication service or utility. There are several interesting cases regarding this which will be discussed below.
